### Research scope 

 Healthcare procurement represents one of the most significant areas of public spending globally, encompassing everything from basic medical consumables to complex pharmaceutical products. This analysis examines procurement patterns across major healthcare markets from 2022 to 2024, focusing on published opportunities that provide visibility into public healthcare purchasing. 

 The data reveals a market dominated by medical consumables and pharmaceutical products, with total published opportunities exceeding 270,000 across the studied period. European markets show the highest visibility with over 163,000 published opportunities, though this reflects standardised publishing requirements rather than necessarily indicating relative market size. 
### Trends 

 Significant trends emerge in both volume and value patterns. Medical consumables demonstrate particular resilience, growing from 178,287 opportunities in 2022 to 227,505 in 2023, with continued strength into 2024. Contract structures show evolution away from framework agreements, whilst duration patterns reveal sophisticated responses to market conditions. 

 This analysis explores these patterns in detail, examining regional variations, category-specific trends, and the relationship between procurement values and contract structures. Throughout, we consider how publishing requirements influence market visibility, ensuring that patterns are interpreted within their proper context.

 The global medical supply market demonstrates significant scale and complexity across the studied period. The total volume of published opportunities shows substantial quarterly variations, with particular strength in first quarters. Medical consumables, the largest category by volume, grew from 178,287 opportunities in 2022 to 227,505 in 2023, whilst various medicinal products maintained more stable volumes around 146,900 opportunities per year until 2024.

### Medical Consumables: Strong and Sustained Growth 

 Medical consumables show the strongest growth of any category in our data. The number of tenders has risen steadily from 89,567 opportunities in 2022 to 128,528 in 2023, reaching 137,759 in 2024. This sustained growth means medical consumables now make up 38.7% of all healthcare contracting opportunities, up from 25.2% in 2022. This increase represents the largest change in market share we have seen across all categories. 
### Medicines: Volume Remains Stable 

 The medicines market, while smaller than consumables, maintains consistent tender volumes. Starting at 48,047 opportunities in 2022, the market saw a brief rise to 54,162 opportunities in 2023 before returning to similar levels with 48,518 opportunities in 2024. Throughout this period, medicines have maintained a steady share of total opportunities at around 32%. 
### Specialist Equipment: Steady Decline 

 Two specialist areas show clear downward trends. Operating equipment opportunities have fallen steadily, starting at 11,820 opportunities in 2022, dropping slightly to 11,755 in 2023, and falling further to 11,060 in 2024. The decline is more pronounced in vaccines and anti-infectives, where tender numbers have dropped consistently from 11,598 opportunities in 2022 to 10,489 in 2023, falling further to 9,708 in 2024. This represents a sustained reduction in opportunities across both specialist sectors. In the case of anti-infectives it is likely that this is the tail end of the pandemic demand curve and represents the new normal for this space. 
### Support Services: Variable Demand 

 Support services show less predictable patterns in the data. From 9,579 opportunities in 2022, the market grew to 11,679 opportunities in 2023, before falling back to 10,363 in 2024. The quarterly data reveals significant variations with some increase each January. This implies that a variety of factors are impacting demand for support services but that the new year and likely release of budget allocations may drive demand for services contracts.

### Overall Value Trends 

 The healthcare procurement market demonstrated significant value fluctuations between 2022 and 2024. Total values peaked notably in 2023, with medical consumables reaching nearly $19 billion in published opportunities, before declining in 2024. However, these figures reflect only opportunities where values were published, typically from European Union member states and select other markets with transparent reporting requirements. 

 The apparent peak in 2023 warrants careful interpretation. Rather than necessarily indicating increased spending, this spike coincides with enhanced reporting practices in several European markets, particularly in France and Germany. The subsequent decline in 2024 similarly reflects changes in reporting patterns rather than definitively indicating reduced procurement activity. 
### Medical Devices Categories Value Analysis 

 The relative stability of medical consumables values, maintaining approximately 40-50% of published values throughout the period, suggests consistent reporting in this category. This steadiness likely stems from standardised reporting requirements for basic medical supplies across European markets. In contrast, various medicinal products showed marked volatility, with values swinging from $14.2 billion in 2022 to $21.1 billion in 2023, before falling to $10.7 billion in 2024. These dramatic shifts often reflect changes in how framework agreements are valued rather than actual spending changes. 

 The miscellaneous devices category, whilst showing smaller absolute values, reveals interesting patterns in procurement strategy. The category’s declining share of total value, from 14.6% in 2022 to 12.1% in 2024, might indicate a trend toward more specific categorisation in procurement rather than necessarily showing reduced spending on devices. 
### Value-Volume Relationships 

 The relationship between procurement values and volumes reveals intriguing patterns that challenge simple interpretation. While medical consumables consistently show high volumes of opportunities, their average values remain relatively modest. This pattern typically reflects decentralised purchasing, where individual hospitals publish separate opportunities for similar items. 

 Conversely, various medicinal products show fewer individual opportunities but often carry higher average values. This disparity largely stems from different purchasing approaches, with medicines more commonly procured through centralised, high-value framework agreements, particularly in Western European markets. 
### Regional Value Distribution 

 European markets dominate published value figures, though this predominantly reflects more comprehensive reporting requirements rather than necessarily indicating larger market sizes. The value distribution across European regions shows marked variations, with Western European opportunities typically carrying higher published values than Eastern European ones, though this partly reflects different approaches to value calculation in framework agreements. 

 North American value data, whilst limited, reveals higher average values per opportunity. This pattern stems from the selective publishing of larger, federal-level opportunities, whilst numerous smaller purchases remain unpublished. Asian and South American markets generally show lower published values, though this primarily reflects different reporting requirements rather than actual market sizes. 
### Value Reporting Analysis 

 Understanding healthcare procurement values requires careful consideration of reporting limitations. Many major healthcare markets, including Japan, South Korea, and much of the United States private sector, operate under reporting regimes that don’t require public value disclosure. Consequently, published values likely represent only a fraction of global healthcare procurement spending. 

 The completeness of value reporting varies significantly by category and region. Whilst basic medical supplies show relatively consistent value reporting, more complex categories like specialist medical devices or pharmaceutical frameworks often have less complete value data. This variation reflects both technical challenges in value estimation and different interpretations of reporting requirements across markets.

### Core Category Performance 

 Our data shows significant value changes across major healthcare categories between 2022 and 2024. Medical consumables and medicinal products consistently dominated the market, together accounting for nearly 60% of all spending in 2022 and 2023. 
### Medical consumables: increased spending 

 Medical consumables spending grew markedly from $15.52 billion in 2022 to $19.05 billion in 2023, a 22.8% increase. However, 2024 saw values decline to $14.40 billion. This mirrors activity in medicinal products, where values rose from $15.55 billion in 2022 to $19.36 billion in 2023, a 24.5% increase, before falling to $12.13 billion in 2024. 

 Looking at quarterly patterns, we see that consumables spending typically peaks in the second quarter of each year. For instance, Q2 2023 saw values 15% higher than the quarterly average for that year. This pattern suggests many healthcare organisations align major consumables purchasing with their financial year end. 
### Medicinal products: aggregating demand 

 The medicinal products category shows less seasonal variation, though we note that the largest contracts, those over $100 million, are more common in the third quarter. Based on the tender descriptions, these often relate to annual framework agreements for hospital pharmacy supplies. 

 In both categories, the average contract value has increased. For medical consumables, the mean contract value rose from $2.8 million in 2022 to $3.4 million in 2024. Similarly, medicinal products contracts grew from an average of $3.1 million to $3.8 million. This suggests buyers are consolidating their purchasing into larger contracts. 
### Changes in Specialist Categories 

 Some smaller categories showed different patterns from the core medical consumables and medicines markets. Functional support spending grew steadily from $2.67 billion in 2022 to $3.72 billion in 2023, maintaining that level through 2024. The average contract value in this category increased more sharply than in other areas, rising from $1.2 million in 2022 to $2.1 million in 2024. The tender descriptions suggest this reflects a move towards more comprehensive support contracts covering multiple services. 
### Imaging equipment centralisation 

 In contrast, imaging equipment spending fell each year, from $2.79 billion in 2022 to $2.26 billion in 2023, and further to $2.04 billion in 2024. However, while total spending decreased, the number of high-value contracts (over $10 million) increased from 12 in 2022 to 18 in 2024. This suggests a trend towards centralised purchasing of imaging equipment, particularly in teaching hospitals and regional health authorities. 
### Dental: significant growth 

 Other specialist categories show varied patterns. Dental and subspecialty instruments saw significant growth, with values rising from $1.8 billion in 2022 to $4.2 billion in 2024. Meanwhile, laboratory equipment and supplies remained relatively stable around $1.5 billion annually, though with notable quarterly fluctuations. 
### Longer term partnerships 

 Looking at contract durations in specialist categories, we see a trend towards longer terms. The average duration for imaging equipment contracts lengthened from 22.9 months in 2022 to 25.5 months in 2024. Similarly, functional support contracts extended from 26.6 months to 29.4 months. This suggests buyers are seeking longer-term partnerships in these technical areas, possibly to ensure consistency of service and support. 
### Market Access Considerations 

 The differences we see in spending patterns likely stem from variations in how different countries publish tender information rather than reflecting actual market sizes. Many healthcare providers are not required to publish opportunity details, while others must publish all purchasing intentions regardless of value.

 Based on the changes we have seen in spending patterns, we think buyers may be moving towards longer-term, higher-value contracts, particularly for everyday items like consumables and medicines. This could create both opportunities and challenges for medical device suppliers, depending on their size and ability to fulfil larger contracts.

### Overview 

 Our analysis of healthcare procurement data from 2022 to 2024 shows notable changes in how long contracts last across different product categories. These changes provide insights into how governments and healthcare organisations are approaching their purchasing strategies.

### Trends in Specialist Equipment 

 The length of contracts varies significantly between different types of medical products and equipment. For example, contracts for cadaver transport and storage equipment have grown steadily longer, increasing from 31 months in 2022 to 38 months in 2023, and reaching 40.2 months in 2024. This suggests buyers may be seeking longer-term stability in this specialist area. 
### Changes in Essential Supplies 

 In contrast, some categories show sharp changes in contract duration. Baby care products contracts halved in length from 48 months in 2023 to 24 months in 2024, after previously running at 40.8 months in 2022. This volatility might indicate changing market conditions or shifting procurement strategies for these essential supplies. 
### Dramatic Shifts in Critical Care Equipment 

 One of the most dramatic changes appears in anaesthesia and resuscitation equipment contracts. The average duration dropped from 34.3 months in 2022 to just 9 months in 2023, and remained short at 8.8 months in 2024. This substantial reduction in contract length might suggest buyers want more flexibility in this category, though we would need more data to understand the full reasons for this change. 
### Core Medical Categories 

 For core medical categories, the patterns are mixed. Medical consumables contracts have fluctuated, moving from 29.4 months in 2022 to 23.3 months in 2023, before lengthening to 31.9 months in 2024. Various medicinal products show a similar pattern, with contract durations falling from 23.4 months to 18.8 months between 2022 and 2023, before increasing to 29.5 months in 2024. 
### Stability in Specialist Areas 

 Some specialist categories maintain more stable contract durations. Clinical forensics equipment contracts showed only a modest change from 36 months in 2022 to 32.8 months in 2024. However, recording systems and exploration devices show a consistent downward trend in contract length, declining from 24.8 months in 2022 to 19.9 months in 2023, and further reducing to 16.1 months in 2024. 
### Business Implications 

 For healthcare solutions providers looking to enter or expand in these markets, these duration patterns offer practical insights for business planning. The variation between categories suggests that different product areas may require different approaches to tender preparation and resource allocation. Companies might need to adapt their strategies based on whether they are operating in categories with lengthening contract durations, like cadaver transport equipment, or those showing shorter terms, like anaesthesia equipment.  Using the data we have gathered we have identified several changes in the healthcare procurement market between 2022 and 2024. By examining the relationships between tender volumes, contract values, and contract durations we can see how buying behaviour has changed. 
### Medical Consumables: Growing Demand and Longer Contracts 

 The data shows that medical consumables buying has grown substantially. The number of tenders increased from 89,567 in 2022 to 137,759 in 2024. During this period, we also saw contract values rise from $15.52 billion in 2022 to $19.05 billion in 2023. Contract durations lengthened from 23.3 months to 31.9 months. This combination of more tenders, higher spending, and longer contracts suggests that buyers might be trying to secure their supply chains for the future. 
### Various Medicinal Products: Fewer, Bigger, Longer Contracts 

 The medicinal products sector shows an interesting pattern. While the number of tenders stayed almost the same (48,047 in 2022 to 48,518 in 2024), the total value of these contracts fell from $19.36 billion in 2023 to $12.13 billion in 2024. At the same time, contract lengths grew from 18.8 months to 29.5 months. These changes suggest that buyers are combining multiple smaller contracts into fewer, longer agreements. This might help them save money through bulk buying. 
### Imaging Equipment: A Shrinking Market 

 The imaging equipment market has become smaller. Both the number of tenders and their value have fallen. Tender numbers dropped from 11,522 in 2022 to 9,883 in 2024, while values decreased from $2.79 billion to $2.04 billion. Unlike other sectors where we see changes in buying methods, this appears to be a straightforward reduction in demand. 
### Functional Support: Higher Values, Shorter Terms 

 The functional support sector shows yet another pattern. While tender numbers fell from 11,679 in 2023 to 10,363 in 2024, the total value stayed steady at around $3.72 billion. Contract lengths shortened slightly from 27.8 months to 26.6 months. This means each tender is worth more money but runs for less time, which might indicate rising prices or larger individual purchases. 
### Miscellaneous Medical Devices: Changing Contract Patterns 

 The data shows that miscellaneous medical devices have gone through significant changes. While tender numbers in 2024 (28,177) are similar to 2022 (28,977), the value of these contracts has changed dramatically. The total value fell from $6.07 billion in 2023 to $3.49 billion in 2024, even as contract lengths grew from 25.7 months to 28.9 months. This suggests buyers are seeking longer-term commitments but at lower prices. 
### Anti-infectives and Vaccines: Changes in Buying Strategy 

 The market for anti-infectives and vaccines has changed notably. Tender numbers fell from 11,598 in 2022 to 9,708 in 2024, while total spending decreased from $3.96 billion to $3.29 billion. At the same time, contract lengths grew from 20.6 months to 27.7 months. This mix of fewer tenders but longer contracts suggests that there is a post-pandemic consolidation with buyers seeking long term, consistent supply. 
### Baby Care Products: Big Changes in Contract Length 

 One of the most striking changes we found was in baby care products. The typical contract length halved from 48 months in 2023 to 24 months in 2024. This is the largest change in contract length we found in our analysis. This suggests buyers have fundamentally changed how they buy these products, perhaps in response to increased commodification of supplies in this market. 
### Operating Techniques: Steady Decline 

 The operating techniques sector shows a different pattern. Unlike other areas which show sharp changes, this sector has declined steadily. Tender numbers fell from 11,820 in 2022 to 11,060 in 2024. This steady decline, rather than sharp changes, might suggest a long-term change in how these products are bought or used. 
### Nervous System Medicines: Sharp Value Changes 

 The data shows that medicines for the nervous system and sensory organs have seen dramatic value changes. The total value of contracts fell from $1.26 billion in 2023 to $566 million in 2024, more than halving in a single year. This significant change in spending might suggest changes in how these medicines are being bought or prescribed.

 The healthcare procurement market shows distinct regional patterns. European buyers account for the highest total value, with dental and subspecialty instruments reaching $26.1 billion in 2024. In comparison, Asian markets show lower total values but higher volumes of opportunities, particularly in medical consumables and anaesthesia equipment, with values around $116.5 million in these categories.

 The data shows that European buyers tend to use longer contracts and framework agreements. These frameworks often run for four years or more. North American buyers focus on specific categories, particularly baby care products and medical consumables, with typical contract lengths between two and three years. Asian markets show more variation in how they buy, using shorter contracts but publishing more frequent opportunities. 
### Worldwide Opportunities 

 Several major organisations buy healthcare products across multiple countries. The World Bank, UNDP, WHO, UNICEF and the International Red Cross create opportunities marked as ‘worldwide’ in our data. These worldwide opportunities often have higher values and longer contract periods than single-country purchases. NATO and EU institutions lead the multinational buying category, typically focusing on strategic products and emergency supplies.
